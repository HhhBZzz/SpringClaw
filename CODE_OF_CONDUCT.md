# Code of Conduct

## Our Standard

SpringClaw welcomes constructive technical collaboration. Contributors are expected to communicate respectfully, focus on the work, and make room for maintainers and users with different levels of context.

Examples of acceptable behavior:

- Giving actionable, specific feedback.
- Explaining technical tradeoffs clearly.
- Respecting project scope and maintainer decisions.
- Disagreeing without personal attacks.

Examples of unacceptable behavior:

- Harassment, insults, or discriminatory language.
- Publishing private information without permission.
- Repeated off-topic or disruptive comments.
- Bad-faith behavior that blocks maintainers from doing project work.

## Enforcement

Maintainers may edit, hide, or remove comments, close issues, block users, or take other reasonable action to protect project quality and contributor safety.
